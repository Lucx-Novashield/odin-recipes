Bolon

Description

The green bolon is a traditional breakfast among the peasants of the coast of Ecuador. The very name of the dish tells us exactly what it is: a ball of plantain which is highly appreciated in that Latin American country.

Ingredients

  • 4 green plantains, peeled and cut into medium pieces.
  • 4-5 tablespoons of butter or lard
  • 2 tablespoons of canola or sunflower oil
  • 1 tablespoon chili pepper of ground chili
  • 1 tablespoon ground cumin
  • 1 cup shredded or grated cheese
  • Salt

Instructions

  1. Add the plantain pieces and cook on medium low heat for about 30-40 minutes or until very soft, turning every 10 minutes. They should be slightly golden, but not crisp.
  2. Sprinkle the pieces of cooked plantains with aji powder or ground chili, cumin and salt to taste.
  3. Put the hot plantain pieces in a large bowl.
  4. Mash the plantains until you get a thick dough.
  5. Shape the douch into small balls, the size should be slightly smaller than a tennis ball.
  6. Make a hole in the center of each ball and fill it with the cheese. Gently press the filling, cover it and shape it back into a ball.
  7. Heat the oil over high heat, add the stuffec plantain balls and fry until golden bwon and crisp on both sides.
  8. Put the fried bolons on a plate covered with absorbent paper to drain the fat and serve immediately.
